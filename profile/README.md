## 🚀 Overview
HWMonitor for Windows is a free, lightweight hardware monitoring tool that shows the vital signs of your PC in a single window. It reads the sensors built into your system to report CPU temperature, GPU temperature, drive temperatures, voltages, fan speeds, and power usage, and it tracks the minimum and maximum values so you can spot spikes over time. The clean, no-frills layout makes it easy to check whether your components are running cool and stable, whether you're diagnosing an issue or simply keeping tabs on a new build. One HWMonitor download gives you a dependable way to watch your hardware's health.

## ✨ Features
- **Temperature Monitoring** — Read CPU, GPU, motherboard, and hard drive temperatures in real time.
- **Voltage & Fan Readouts** — Keep track of system voltages, fan speeds, and power consumption.
- **Min/Max Logging** — See the lowest and highest values recorded during your session to catch spikes.
- **Broad Sensor Support** — Works with a wide range of chips, mainboards, and components.
- **Lightweight & Simple** — A small, easy-to-read interface that shows everything at a glance.
- **Portable Option** — A portable build lets you run it without installing when you prefer.

## ❓ FAQ
**Is HWMonitor free?**
Yes. HWMonitor is free to download and use for monitoring your hardware. A separate HWMonitor Pro version is also available with extra features for users who want more.

**Is HWMonitor safe?**
Yes. HWMonitor is a legitimate, read-only monitoring tool that reports the values from your system's sensors. It doesn't change your settings or overclock anything — it simply displays your hardware's temperatures, voltages, and fan speeds. As always, download it from the official source to be sure you get the genuine app.

**What is the difference between HWMonitor and HWMonitor Pro?**
The standard HWMonitor is free and shows your sensor readings in a simple window. HWMonitor Pro is a paid upgrade that adds features such as graphs, remote monitoring, and a customizable interface for users who need more.

**Which versions of Windows are supported?**
HWMonitor runs on modern versions of Windows, including Windows 10 and Windows 11. Keeping Windows and your device drivers up to date helps it read your sensors accurately.
